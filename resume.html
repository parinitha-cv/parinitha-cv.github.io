The use of Speech for Parkinson’s Disease detection

In this study, I conducted preprocessing and exploratory data analysis on a Parkinson's Disease dataset. I visualized the data using various plots, analyzed the distribution and outliers through distplots and boxplots, and examined correlations using a heatmap. Following that, I performed data cleaning and preprocessing steps, separating independent and dependent variables and addressing class imbalance through resampling techniques. I then trained an XGBoost classifier on the preprocessed dataset, splitting it into training and testing sets. Model evaluation was carried out, assessing accuracy and other metrics such as precision, recall, F1-score, and ROC curves. Based on the evaluation results, the best-performing models were selected, and hyperparameter tuning was performed to improve their performance. Finally, the best model was deployed in a production environment using Flask to create a web application for customer churn prediction. The models achieved high accuracy and F1-scores, demonstrating their effectiveness in predicting customer churn. Recommendations for further improvement included hyperparameter tuning and feature selection.

Social Media Influencer with an AI-Generated Persona:
The Department of Surrey Police

This internship aimed to attempt to build a social media influencer with an AI generated persona. We utilized our knowledge of transfer models (GPT- 4), DALL-E, Gan’s, and style GAN’s to be able to achieve these deliverables. As an experiment, I worked with CarynAI and Kyra, notable examples of AI companions and virtual influencers. CarynAI allows two-way audio conversations with an AI clone of a social media influencer, while Kyra is a fictional virtual influencer with a significant following. They showcase the transformative impact of AI in influencer marketing and virtual companionship, offering immersive experiences and cost-effective collaborations. Audio training platforms like Deepgram enhance the generation of synchronized audio for AI-generated images. The future holds great potential for AI companions and virtual influencers.

Provided an overview of various AI tools and models used for generating deepfakes in different formats, including text, images, videos, and audio. Some notable models discussed include ChatGPT, Surfer, Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, AI Image Generator by Fotor, Synthesia, Pictory, Reface app, Tacotron, and FineShare FineVoice.

The performance and requirements of each model are highlighted, along with their strengths and limitations. We emphasize that AI-generated images often have certain artifacts and errors, such as incorrect hand and finger drawings, blurred backgrounds, and unnatural eye placement. Producing convincing deepfake videos and audio is more challenging, as they tend to sound robotic and have unnatural movements. We also suggest some tricks to enhance deepfake generation, such as using the edit feature in DALL-E to modify images, generating images with consistent seeds, being specific in prompts, and mapping fake faces onto pre-recorded videos.

Murmur Detection - George B. Moody PhysioNet Challenge 2022.

In my study, I focused on classifying abnormal heart data to detect heart murmurs and predict clinical outcomes. I explored two approaches: a Machine Learning approach using models like Random Forest, Kmeans, and Logistic Regression, and a Deep Neural Network Architecture.

To pre-process the heart sound recordings, I divided them into overlapping parts and converted them into logarithmic spectrograms. The architecture integrated feature maps from different streams and evaluated performance using metrics such as AUROC, AU PRC, F-measure, and accuracy on validation and test datasets.
